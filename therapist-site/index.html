Who I Help

Thoughtful therapy for men, veterans, and adults navigating anxiety, identity, and change.

My clients are often capable, responsible, and deeply committed to the people around them. On the outside, they function well. On the inside, they feel exhausted, disconnected, or stuck in cycles they can’t explain.

Real therapy for people tired of performing strength instead of feeling it.

Common starting points

  • Persistent anxiety, overthinking, and emotional tension
  • Burnout, numbness, and loss of motivation
  • Identity shifts, career uncertainty, and life transitions
  • Relationship conflict and difficulty expressing emotion
  • Shame, self-criticism, and financial stress tied to self-worth

Core struggles clients bring into therapy

Anxiety and pressure

Constant vigilance, racing thoughts, and the feeling that rest has to be earned.

Emotional disconnection

Knowing what to do intellectually, but feeling distant from your own emotions and needs.

Relationships under strain

Repeating the same conflict patterns, withdrawing, or struggling to communicate honestly.

Identity and purpose

Questioning who you are now, what matters, and how to live in alignment rather than autopilot.

Shame and avoidance

Hiding pain behind productivity, sarcasm, anger, or staying endlessly busy.

Financial stress and worth

Carrying silent pressure around money, performance, and what your value is tied to.

My approach: compassionate, direct, and grounded in real life.

I offer therapy that is emotionally attuned and practical. We won’t stay on the surface. We’ll slow down enough to understand what’s driving your patterns, then translate that insight into how you live, communicate, and lead your life.

I integrate psychodynamic depth, attachment awareness, and evidence-based strategies in a way that feels clear and useful.

Why work with me

  • Warm, high-trust presence with clear direction
  • Depth-oriented work without losing practicality
  • Strong fit for men and veterans seeking honest therapy
  • Respectful challenge when old defenses are no longer serving you

Simple process

1. Consultation

A brief call to understand what you’re navigating, answer questions, and assess fit.

2. Intake

Your first full session where we map your story, current stressors, and treatment goals.

3. Ongoing therapy

Consistent sessions focused on measurable change, emotional depth, and lasting integration.
